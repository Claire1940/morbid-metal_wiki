## Gameplay Mechanics and Character Shifting

The core appeal of any **morbid metal console version** will be its unique shapeshifting mechanic. Unlike other roguelikes where you pick one character for a run, here you can swap between different "Forms" on the fly, each with distinct move sets and cooldowns.

### The Character Roster

1.  **Flux:** The speed specialist. Flux is designed for players who want to overwhelm enemies with a flurry of strikes. His mobility makes him the best choice for dodging large AoE attacks.
2.  **Echo:** The heavy hitter. While slower, Echo can break enemy poise and deal massive burst damage. Swapping to Echo when an enemy is stunned is a common high-level strategy.
3.  **Experimental Forms:** As the game progresses through 2026, additional forms are being added, each offering a new "special skill" that can turn the tide of battle.

> **Warning:** Be aware of the "Invisibility Bug" currently present in Early Access. Occasionally, switching characters too rapidly can cause your model to disappear momentarily. This is expected to be patched long before the console launch.

## Progression and Combat Strategy

Success in the game relies on mastering the synergy between your forms and the upgrades you find during a run. The skill-based nature of the combat means that even with poor RNG on upgrades, a skilled player can survive through precise dodging.

### The "Infinite Backstab" Strategy

A popular strategy discovered during the 2026 Early Access phase involves the "Backstab" skill. By focusing your upgrades on critical hit damage and "Kill Reset" modifiers, you can create a chain reaction.

*   **Step 1:** Acquire the Backstab skill.
*   **Step 2:** Find the "Execute" upgrade, which resets the skill cooldown if it kills an enemy.
*   **Step 3:** Stack Critical Chance to ensure the first hit kills the weakest enemy.
*   **Step 4:** Activate the skill to automatically zip between enemies, clearing entire rooms of "trash mobs" in seconds.

| Upgrade Type | Priority | Effect |

| **Speed Boost** | Medium | Helps Flux maintain momentum |
