## Navigating the Iterations: The Roguelite Loop

Every time you die in Morbid Metal, you return to "The Void." This serves as your central hub where you can interact with various protocols to strengthen your characters for the next "Iteration." The levels themselves are procedural, meaning the layout, enemy spawns, and loot will change every time you begin a new run.

### Permanent Upgrades
To progress through the more difficult biomes, such as the Courtyard or the Industrial Sectors, you must spend **Void Matter**. This currency is collected by defeating enemies and completing room challenges.

1.  **Max Health Increase:** Essential for surviving the heavy-hitting bosses in later iterations.
2.  **Perfect Dodge Counter:** Unlocks the ability to perform a powerful strike immediately after a well-timed dodge.
3.  **Soul Cores:** Used to unlock entirely new robotic forms to add to your shapeshifting rotation.
4.  **Routine Enhancements:** Permanent buffs to your skill cooldowns and movement speed.

| Upgrade Type | Cost (Void Matter) | Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Health +10%** | 20 | **High** |
| **Dodge Counter** | 15 | **Medium** |
| **Skill Cooldown** | 30 | **High** |

> **Warning:** Some rooms feature timer-based challenges where you must destroy all enemies before the clock runs out. If you fail, you may lose the rewards for that specific sector.

## Exploration and Hidden Secrets

While the combat is fast-paced, Morbid Metal rewards players who take a moment to explore the environment. Procedural levels often hide materials and "Routines" that can be traded with NPCs like Eden.

*   **Yellow Currency:** Keep an eye out for glowing yellow materials. These are often used for temporary run-based buffs rather than permanent upgrades.
*   **Hidden Platforms:** Use your double jump and dash to reach high-up ledges. You might find health pickups or extra Void Matter tucked away in the corners of the map.
*   **Environmental Cues:** Different colored flora or structures often indicate what kind of loot or enemy type is nearby.

## Technical Setup for Xbox Players on PC

Since many are looking for the **morbid metal xbox game pass** experience on their computers, setting up the controls correctly is vital. The game is designed for high-precision movement, which can be tricky on a standard mouse and keyboard if you are used to a gamepad.

If you are using an Xbox controller:
*   **Enable Auto-Sprint:** This allows you to focus on dodging and attacking without constantly holding down the thumbstick.
*   **Toggle Lock-On:** Use the right stick click to stay focused on larger enemies, which is crucial for landing Blink Strikes.
*   **Elite Controller Mapping:** If you have back paddles, map your dash and character swap buttons to them to keep your thumbs on the sticks at all times.
