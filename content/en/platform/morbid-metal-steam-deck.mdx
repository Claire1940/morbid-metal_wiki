## Leveraging Lossless Scaling for 60 FPS

One of the most effective ways to play **morbid metal steam deck** at a consistent 60 FPS is by employing the "Lossless Scaling" tool available on [Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/993090/Lossless_Scaling/). While the game doesn't feature built-in frame generation, this external utility can bridge the gap.

When using Lossless Scaling, you can actually afford to bump your in-game settings to the **Medium Preset**. This allows for a much cleaner visual experience while the scaling tool handles the frame interpolation. Surprisingly, even in a game this fast, the added latency from Lossless Scaling is minimal and often unnoticeable during standard gameplay.

### Steps to Configure Lossless Scaling:
1. Launch the game and set it to **Windowed Mode** at 1280x800 or 1024x640.
2. Open the Lossless Scaling app.
3. Set the Scaling Mode to "Auto" and the Scaling Type to "LS1" or "FSR".
4. Enable **Frame Generation (LSFG)**.
5. Return to the game and trigger the scaling shortcut.

## Combat and Gameplay Mechanics on Deck

Playing **morbid metal steam deck** feels very similar to high-speed character action games. You take control of various "Metal" forms, each with unique abilities and weapons. The core mechanic involves switching between these forms mid-combo to exploit enemy weaknesses.

On the Steam Deck's smaller screen, keeping track of these transformations is relatively easy, but the intense particle effects during boss fights can occasionally obscure incoming attacks. Because you can die very easily, it is vital to have the back buttons (L4/L5/R4/R5) mapped to your dodge and transformation keys. This allows you to keep your thumbs on the sticks at all times, which is essential for the 360-degree combat environment.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The game is currently in Early Access. You may encounter occasional crashes or UI bugs when waking the Steam Deck from sleep mode. It is recommended to save at checkpoints frequently.
