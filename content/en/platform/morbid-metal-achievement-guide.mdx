## Combat & Shifting Achievements

The core mechanic of the game is "shifting"—the ability to switch characters mid-combat to extend combos or avoid damage. Mastering this is the key to the **morbid metal achievement guide**'s most technical trophies.

### Shifting Mastery
To unlock the *Master of Forms* achievement, you must perform a 50-hit combo that includes at least one shift to every unlocked character. This is best attempted in the second biome, where enemies have enough health to survive a long chain of attacks but aren't dangerous enough to interrupt you instantly.

### Environmental Kills
The *Scrap Metal* achievement requires you to kill 100 enemies using environmental hazards. Look for explosive canisters and falling debris in the industrial zones. Using the Colossus’s knockback abilities is the most effective way to push enemies into these traps.

## Boss Trophies and Tactics

Bosses in this game are massive, multi-phase encounters that test your ability to read telegraphs. There are several achievements tied to these encounters, ranging from simple victories to perfect, no-damage runs.

1. **The First Gatekeeper**: Defeat the tutorial boss without using a health repair kit.
2. **The Core Processor**: Destroy the central core in the fourth biome within 3 minutes of the fight starting.
3. **Perfect Machine**: Defeat any major boss without taking a single hit.

> **Warning:** Attempting "Perfect Machine" is best done with the Shadow character, as their dash has the most generous invincibility frames (i-frames) in the game.

## Secret and Hidden Achievements

There are a handful of hidden achievements that won't appear in your list until you trigger them. Most of these are related to Easter eggs or highly specific death scenarios.

*   **System Failure**: Die within the first 60 seconds of a fresh run.
*   **The Architect's Secret**: Find the hidden developer room in the final biome (behind a destructible wall near the boss elevator).
*   **Overclocked**: Maintain the "Overdrive" state for a total of 5 minutes across a single run.

Mastering the "Overclocked" achievement requires a build focused on energy retention and rapid kills. Using the Spark character is highly recommended here, as their passive abilities naturally generate more energy per hit.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I complete the morbid metal achievement guide in a single run?**

A: No, it is impossible to get every achievement in one run. You will need multiple playthroughs to unlock all characters, max out the upgrade tree, and complete the "No-Hit" challenges for different bosses. Expect to spend at least 25-30 hours for a full 100% completion.

**Q: What is the hardest achievement in Morbid Metal?**

A: Most players find *Untouchable God* to be the hardest. It requires you to finish the entire final biome and the final boss without taking any damage. This requires perfect knowledge of every enemy spawn and boss phase.

**Q: Are there any missable achievements?**

A: Because the game is a roguelike, nothing is truly "missable" in the traditional sense. You can always start a new run to go after a specific trophy. However, some achievements are much easier to get before you upgrade your damage too high, as you need enemies to survive long enough for high-hit combos.

**Q: Does difficulty affect achievement unlocks?**

A: Yes, certain achievements like *True Metal* require you to beat the game on the highest difficulty setting. Lower difficulties are great for practicing boss patterns, but you will eventually need to crank up the challenge for the final few trophies.
