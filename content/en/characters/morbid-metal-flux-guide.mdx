## Understanding Flux’s Core Abilities

Flux is the cornerstone of your team, acting as the most versatile unit for both crowd control and single-target DPS. Unlike the heavy-hitting Echo, Flux relies on "Blink Strikes" and "Uniform Blades" to maintain pressure from various ranges. Mastering his movement is the first step toward conquering the higher iterations of the simulation.

| Ability Name | Type | Description | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blink Strike** | Gap Closer | Instantly propels Flux toward a locked-on enemy for a slash. | Closing distance on flying drones. |
| **Uniform Blades** | Ranged | Launches lethal energy blades from a distance. | Safely chipping away at boss health. |
| **Lingering Blades** | Area Denial | Deploys energy fields that damage enemies over time. | Controlling groups of shield-bearing mobs. |
| **Deflect** | Defensive | A timed block that parries incoming melee attacks. | Staggering heavy "Juggernaut" enemies. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always prioritize taking out flying drones first. Their projectiles are the most common cause of "Rank" loss and can interrupt your flow during intense ground skirmishes.

## Essential Protocols and Routines for Flux

As you progress through a run, you will encounter "Protocols" (active skills) and "Routines" (passive buffs). For a successful Flux build, you want to focus on Critical Hit synergy and health sustain. Since Flux has a lower health pool than other units, the "Gluttony" routine—which restores health on kills—is almost mandatory for long-distance runs.

### Recommended Protocol Upgrades
1.  **Nanoblades Enhancement:** Focus on reducing the cooldown of your regular skills. This allows you to spam Uniform Blades, keeping you safe while dealing consistent damage.
2.  **Executioner’s Slash:** An upgrade that instantly kills enemies below a certain health threshold. This is vital for clearing out heavy shield-users quickly.
3.  **Critical Surge:** Every critical hit sends out additional blades, creating a screen-clearing effect when paired with high attack speed.

## Managing Resources: Nanochips and Vitality

In Morbid Metal, your primary currency is Nanochips. These are dropped by defeated enemies and found in breakable environmental objects. You must decide whether to spend these at Vitality Fabricators for health or save them for permanent skill tree upgrades back at the hub.

| Resource Type | Acquisition Method | Primary Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Nanochips** | Combat & Exploration | Purchasing heals and upgrading Protocols mid-run. |
| **Soul Cores** | Boss Drops | Unlocking new characters (like Echo) and permanent stat boosts. |
| **Vitality** | Fabricators / Routines | Keeping your character alive through multiple phases. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Some altars allow you to "Convert Health into Nanochips." Never do this if your health is below 50%, as the game’s difficulty spikes significantly after the first boss.

## Boss Strategy: Defeating Uriel

Uriel serves as the first major skill check in your journey. He is a multi-phase boss that utilizes massive area-of-effect (AoE) slams and high-speed dashes. To beat him using this **morbid metal flux guide**, you must master the jump-dash timing.

### Uriel Phase Breakdown

*   **Phase 1:** Uriel uses basic ground slams. Stay close, get 2-3 hits in, and dash away when he raises his arms. He will occasionally jump toward you; time your jump to avoid the shockwave.
*   **Phase 2:** Uriel becomes more aggressive and introduces a triple-slash wave. You must dash through the waves or stay at maximum range using Uniform Blades.
*   **Phase 3:** The most dangerous phase. Uriel charges a "Void Orb" that can take off 50% of your health instantly. Do not touch the glowing areas on the ground. Focus on range and only go in for a melee strike when he "beats his chest" (his stagger window).

## Advanced Tips for High-Rank Runs

Maintaining a "S" Rank in combat isn't just for show; it increases your Nanochip drop rate and improves the quality of Routines offered at the end of a stage. To keep your rank high, you must avoid taking damage at all costs.

1.  **Use the Environment:** Many levels have verticality. Use your grapple (C key) to escape ground-based enemies and reset your cooldowns.
2.  **Synergize with Echo:** Once you unlock Echo, use Flux to build up the combo meter and then swap to Echo to deliver the finishing blow. Echo’s heavy attacks benefit greatly from the "Stagger" status Flux can apply.
3.  **Prioritize Attack Speed:** In the 2026 meta, attack speed is king. The faster Flux swings, the more chances you have to trigger "Critical Blade" procs, which can melt elite enemies in seconds.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I unlock the second character, Echo?**

A: Echo is unlocked by progressing through the "Sublime Gardens" and reaching the encounter where he appears. In the current 2026 version, you don't necessarily have to defeat him in a traditional boss fight; following the narrative path will eventually grant you his Soul Core.

**Q: What is the best Routine to pick in this morbid metal flux guide?**

A: "Gluttony" is widely considered the best Routine for beginners as it restores a percentage of max health per kill. For advanced players, "Critical Surge" provides the highest DPS output.

**Q: Does the game have checkpoints?**

A: No. Morbid Metal is a true roguelike. If you die, you return to the beginning of the simulation (Iteration 1), though you keep any permanent upgrades purchased at the hub using Soul Cores.

**Q: Can I destroy the environment for health?**

A: Yes, certain elements like crates and glowing canisters can contain small health pickups or Nanochips. However, these are rare, so relying on the Vitality Fabricator is a safer bet.
