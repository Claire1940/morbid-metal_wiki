## Mastering In-Game Platforming and Movement

The **morbid metal platforms** within the simulation are designed to test your agility. Unlike traditional hack-and-slash titles that focus purely on grounded combat, *Morbid Metal* incorporates verticality and environmental hazards that can be just as deadly as the robotic sentinels hunting you.

### Movement Mechanics
Players have access to a versatile suite of movement tools. The double jump is your primary method for scaling heights, while the sprint allows for rapid repositioning. Grapple nodes are scattered throughout the biomes, allowing for quick traversal across gaps. However, it is important to note that grappling is primarily a traversal tool and is rarely used directly in active combat zones.

### Environmental Hazards
Each biome introduces new hazards that require precise timing. You will encounter spinning lasers, floor traps, and disappearing **morbid metal platforms**. The penalty for failing a platforming section is typically a loss of health, which can be devastating in a rogue-lite where healing items are scarce.

| Hazard Type | Effect | Best Counter |
|---|---|---|
| **Spinning Lasers** | High energy damage over time | Time your dash to phase through the beam |
| **Corrosive Floors** | Slows movement and ticks health | Use double jump and air-dashes to stay airborne |

## Character Switching: The Core Gameplay Pillar

In *Morbid Metal*, you don't just pick one class; you build a squad. You can control up to three different characters, switching between them instantly to chain attacks and manage cooldowns. This "platform" of interchangeable playstyles is what sets the game apart from its peers.

### The Starting Roster
1.  **Flux:** The katana-wielding specialist. Flux is built for speed, utilizing teleporting stabs and mid-range slicing projectiles. He is the ideal choice for 1-on-1 duels and closing the distance on ranged enemies.
2.  **Iku (Ekko):** The powerhouse. Iku focuses on heavy hits and crowd control. His primary role is launching enemies into the air and stunning them, setting up devastating combos for the rest of the team.
3.  **Vecta:** The tactician. Vecta offers advanced crowd control, capable of pulling and pushing enemies. His "Link" status effect allows damage dealt to one enemy to be shared across all linked targets, making him essential for clearing large waves.

| Character | Role | Key Ability | Synergy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Flux** | Assassin | Teleporting Stab | Follow up after Iku's air launch |
| **Iku** | Bruiser | Air Smash | Sets up Vecta's ground traps |
| **Vecta** | Controller | Damage Link | Amplifies Flux's single-target DPS |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not rely on a single character for the entire run. The "Leak" status effect (similar to bleed) stacks up to nine times, but it is most effective when applied through rapid character cycling to maximize ability uptime.

## Progression and the Void Nexus

Between runs, you return to "The Void," the game's central hub. This is where you spend the currency earned during your attempts to permanently upgrade your AI's capabilities. The progression system in 2026 is deep, allowing for significant customization of your starting abilities and passive stats.

### The Void Nexus
The Void Nexus is the heart of your long-term progression. Here, you can spend Void Matter to increase:
*   **Basic Attack Damage:** Increases the floor of your DPS.
*   **Attack Speed:** Vital for stacking status effects like Leak.
*   **Critical Damage:** Essential for late-game builds that rely on high-burst damage.
*   **Health and Shield Capacity:** Increases your survivability across the various **morbid metal platforms** and biomes.

### Enhancers and Curses
During a run, you will encounter Enhancers that provide temporary buffs. However, some of the most powerful rewards come from "monkey paw" deals—curses that offer massive power at a significant cost. Balancing these risks is the key to reaching the final biomes of the simulation.

## Combat Strategy and Boss Encounters

The combat in *Morbid Metal* is designed to be punishing but fair. Utilizing the "Style Meter" is crucial, as a higher style rank can actually influence your ability cooldowns, allowing for more frequent use of ultimates.

### Boss Mechanics
Bosses are multi-phase encounters that test everything you have learned. They often utilize split-second projectile patterns and summon connected minions that must be destroyed to lower the boss's shields. In 2026, the boss roster has expanded to include diverse mechanical threats, each requiring a specific team composition to conquer efficiently.

1.  **Phase 1: Observation.** Focus on learning the dodge timings for their primary projectiles.
2.  **Phase 2: Aggression.** Use Vecta to link the boss to any summoned minions to double your damage output.
3.  **Phase 3: Execution.** Save Flux's ultimate for the final 20% of the boss's health to phase through their most dangerous "desperation" attacks.

## FAQ

**Q: What are the primary morbid metal platforms I can play on in 2026?**

A: Currently, *Morbid Metal* is primarily available on PC via Steam. It is fully optimized for Windows 10/11 and runs exceptionally well on high-end gaming handhelds like the Steam Deck and ROG Ally with adjusted settings.

**Q: How do I unlock new characters in the simulation?**

A: You start with Flux. Iku (Ekko) is unlocked shortly after completing the first biome's trials, while Vecta is typically found deeper in the simulation after defeating the second major boss.

**Q: Is there a way to heal during a run?**

A: Healing items are found sporadically in out-of-the-way areas that require minor platforming to reach. Additionally, some "Corporas" (passive stat items) can provide health regeneration or life-steal under specific combat conditions.

**Q: What is the "Leak" status effect, and why is it important?**

A: Leak is a status effect similar to bleed. It can stack up to nine times, dealing damage over time and increasing the damage the enemy takes from all sources. It is one of the most powerful tools for "phasing" bosses quickly.
