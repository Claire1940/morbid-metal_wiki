The 2026 launch introduces a refined roster of drones, each designed to fill a specific niche in your combat rotation. ### The Vanguard (Melee Specialist)
The Vanguard is your bread-and-butter combatant. It excels at close-quarters engagement and features a high-frequency blade capable of parrying most projectile attacks. In the current version of the game, the Vanguard has received new talent trees that focus on "Impact Damage," allowing it to stagger even the largest bosses.

### The Sentinel (Defense & Crowd Control)
When the screen becomes cluttered with enemy fire, the Sentinel is your best friend. This form possesses the highest armor rating and can deploy temporary shields. Its primary utility in the **morbid metal new content** is its ability to pull enemies together, setting them up for a high-damage swap-attack from another drone.

### The Ghost (Speed & Precision)
The Ghost is designed for players who value glass-cannon builds. With the ability to dash through enemies and leave behind explosive decoys, the Ghost can navigate the battlefield faster than any other form. However, its low health pool makes it a risky choice for sustained combat.

## Advanced Combat Mechanics and Synergies

Combat in this game is built on the "Active Swap" system. When you switch characters during an attack animation, the incoming drone performs a "Swap Strike." This mechanic is the cornerstone of high-level play in the **morbid metal new content**. By timing your swaps correctly, you can maintain a constant flow of aggression while bypassing the cooldown periods of individual drone abilities.

The environmental hazards in the 2026 update also play a larger role. You will encounter electrified floors, toxic vents, and crumbling platforms. Drones like the Ghost can bypass these hazards with ease, while the Sentinel can often ignore the damage entirely for a short duration.

1.  **Stagger Management**: Watch the yellow bar beneath an enemy's health. Filling this bar will temporarily stun them.
2.  **Elemental Priming**: Some drones apply "Status Effects" (like Freeze or Burn). Swapping to a different drone can trigger an "Elemental Burst" for massive bonus damage.
3.  **Aerial Combat**: Many new enemies are airborne. Use the Vanguard's grapple or the Stalker's anti-air rounds to bring them down.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Swapping drones consumes a small portion of your "Energy Bar." If you exhaust this bar, you will be locked into your current form until it recharges.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I unlock new drones in the morbid metal new content?**

A: New drones are typically unlocked by defeating specific "Gatekeeper" bosses at the end of each biome. Once defeated, you can purchase the drone's core in the Hub using the resources gathered during that run.

**Q: Is there a limit to how many times I can swap drones during combat?**

A: There is no hard limit on the number of swaps, but each swap consumes "Swap Energy." If your energy bar is empty, you must wait for it to regenerate before you can switch forms again.

**Q: Can I play Morbid Metal offline in 2026?**

A: Yes, the Early Access version supports full offline play. However, you will need to be online to download the latest patches and sync your progress with the Steam Cloud.

**Q: What happens to my progress when a new update is released?**

A: The developers aim to maintain save game compatibility throughout the Early Access period. However, major overhauls to the skill tree may result in a "Respec," where your points are returned to you to be spent on the new systems.
