## Character Classes and Combat Mechanics

In *Morbid Metal*, you don't just play as one warrior; you command a squad of AI entities that you can cycle through in real-time. This "form-switching" mechanic is the core of the gameplay, allowing for devastating combos and tactical flexibility. Each character has a unique identity and skill set that can be further customized in the "Void" hub.

### The Playable Warriors

1.  **Flux:** Your starting character. Flux is a katana-wielding specialist focused on agility. His kit revolves around teleporting stabs and mid-range slicing projectiles. He is perfect for players who prefer a "high-speed, low-drag" approach to combat.
2.  **Iku:** A powerhouse playstyle that joins the roster shortly after the tutorial. Iku specializes in stunning enemies and launching them into the air, dealing massive impact damage.
3.  **Vecta:** The crowd-control expert. Vecta can pull and push enemies across the battlefield and inflict the "Link" status, which causes damage to be shared among all connected foes.

### Synergizing Your Build

The real depth of the combat comes from combining these characters. For instance, you can use Iku to launch a group of enemies into the air and then immediately switch to Flux to deliver a flurry of mid-air slashes. The "Leak" status effect is another critical component of high-level play. This effect acts as a stacking bleed, dealing damage over time and increasing the vulnerability of the target. When upgraded, Leak can stack up to nine times, allowing players to outright "phase" bosses by bypassing their defensive mechanics through pure attrition.

## Progression and The Void Hub

Between runs, players return to "The Void," a de facto hub where the "Void Nexus" allows for permanent upgrades. This is where the rogue-lite elements truly shine, as you use currency gathered during your runs to bolster your AI's capabilities for 2026 and beyond.

*   **Void Matter:** The primary currency used to upgrade basic stats like attack damage, critical hit chance, and movement speed.
*   **Ability Alterations:** You can spend resources to change the fundamental behavior of your characters' skills. For example, Vecta’s ultimate can be changed from a stationary spin to a levitation field that traps all nearby enemies.
*   **Enhancers:** These are passive buffs that can be unlocked and equipped to provide benefits between biomes, such as increased healing from drops or higher air-damage modifiers.

> **💡 Tip:** Prioritize upgrading "Attack Speed" and "Critical Damage" in the Void Nexus early on. Because the combat relies on fast-stacking status effects like Leak, faster attacks lead to exponentially higher DPS.

## Exploring the Biomes: Trial and Error

The level design in *Morbid Metal* takes heavy inspiration from modern sci-fi action games. The environments are filled with grapple nodes, spinning lasers, and platforming hazards that require precise movement. Currently, there are three biomes available in the Early Access version:

1.  **The Industrial Simulation:** A gritty, metallic landscape that serves as the introduction to the game's mechanics.
2.  **The Overgrown Bio-Dome:** This area introduces more complex platforming and verticality, along with hazardous foliage.
3.  **The Core:** A high-intensity zone that tests everything you have learned, culminating in difficult boss encounters.

The game features "Trials" within these levels—timed challenges where you must survive for a set duration or switch forms every five seconds to earn high-tier payouts. These trials are high-risk, high-reward, often providing the "Corpora" (passive stat gains) needed to survive the later stages of a run.
