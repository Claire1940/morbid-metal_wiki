## Meet the Shape-Shifting Robots

One of the primary reasons players are willing to pay the **morbid metal price** is the unique character-swapping mechanic. Unlike traditional rogue-likes where you stick to a single build, Morbid Metal allows you to cycle through different "forms" or robots in real-time. This creates a tactical layer where you can initiate a combo with a fast attacker and finish it with a heavy hitter.

### Flux: The Katana Specialist
Flux is your starting character and the face of the game. Specializing in high-speed mobility and precision strikes, Flux utilizes "Nanoblades" to shred enemies.
*   **Strengths:** Teleporting stabs, mid-range projectiles, and superior dodge frames.
*   **Best For:** Boss encounters and 1v1 duels where timing is everything.

### Iku: The Heavy Powerhouse
Iku offers a distinct shift in momentum. Where Flux is a scalpel, Iku is a sledgehammer.
*   **Strengths:** Massive AOE (Area of Effect) damage, stunning enemies, and launching foes into the air.
*   **Best For:** Crowd control and breaking the shields of elite "bulwark" enemies.

### Vecta: The Crowd Controller
Vecta arrives later in the run and focuses on utility and status manipulation.
*   **Strengths:** Pulling and pushing enemies, inflicting the "Link" status for shared damage.
*   **Best For:** Managing large groups of weaker enemies in the more chaotic simulation trials.

## Combat Mechanics: Mastering the Simulation

The combat in Morbid Metal is heavily skill-based, rewarding players who learn enemy patterns rather than those who simply mash buttons. The "snappy" feel of the controls is a major selling point that justifies the **morbid metal price** for action game veterans.

### Animation Canceling and Perfect Dodges
A standout feature is the ability to animation cancel basic attacks with a dodge. This means you are never "locked" into a bad move. If you see a giant red indicator over an enemy's head, you can immediately dash to trigger a perfect dodge counter, which slows down time and allows for a devastating follow-up.

### The "Leak" Status Effect
Currently, the most powerful mechanic in the game is the **Leak** status. Functioning similarly to "Bleed" in other RPGs, Leak can be upgraded to stack up to nine times.
*   **Effect:** Deals damage over time and increases the damage the enemy takes from all other sources.
*   **Strategy:** By cycling through Flux, Iku, and Vecta and applying abilities rapidly, you can "phase" bosses, skipping their more difficult attack patterns entirely.

| Mechanic | Description | Tactical Advantage |
|---|---|---|
| **Perfect Dodge** | Dashing exactly as an enemy attack flashes red. | Triggers split-second slowdown and counter-attack. |
| **Character Swap** | Pressing 1, 2, or 3 to change forms mid-combo. | Maintains combo momentum and covers elemental weaknesses. |
| **Grapple Hook** | Using nodes to zip across the arena. | Essential for avoiding ground-based AOE attacks and lasers. |
| **Void Matter** | Meta-progression currency earned during runs. | Used to permanently upgrade base stats like crit damage. |

## Progression and Meta-Upgrades

While the combat is the hook, the rogue-lite loop is what keeps players coming back. After paying the **morbid metal price**, you'll spend most of your time in the "Void Nexus," the game's central hub. Here, you can spend Void Matter to enhance your robots permanently.

### The Void Nexus Upgrades
1.  **Basic Attack Damage:** Increases the floor of your DPS across all characters.
2.  **Attack Speed:** Vital for Flux to trigger status effects faster.
3.  **Critical Damage:** High-risk, high-reward path for advanced players.
4.  **Ability Permutations:** Unlocking alternate ultimates, such as Vecta’s levitation field versus her spinning blade.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful with "Monkey Paw" deals found during runs. These modifiers offer massive power boosts but come with severe penalties, such as reduced health or increased enemy speed.

## Performance and Technical Specs

For many PC gamers, the **morbid metal price** is only part of the equation; performance is the other half. The game is built on Unity and features impressive lighting and particle effects that mimic a dark, sci-fi aesthetic.

*   **Optimization:** The game supports DLSS (Deep Learning Super Sampling). On an RTX 4060 at 1080p/1440p, players can expect stable frame rates even during intense combat encounters.
*   **Visuals:** While some "pop-in" of foliage has been noted in the second biome, the character models and animations are fluid and high-quality.
*   **Hardware Recommendation:** A minimum of 16GB of RAM and an i5-11400 (or equivalent) is recommended to maintain the "snappy" feel essential for perfect dodges.
