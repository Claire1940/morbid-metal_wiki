## Understanding the Shapeshifting Mechanic

The core of the gameplay involves managing a squad of up to four different "Metal" forms. Unlike traditional RPGs where you stick to one class, this title encourages you to swap characters mid-combo to extend your offensive pressure or mitigate incoming damage. Each form has a unique energy meter and cooldown period, making the timing of your transitions critical.

| Mechanic | Function | Strategic Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **Active Swap** | Instantly switch between 4 forms | Extends combos and cancels recovery frames |
| **Burst Transition** | A shockwave emitted during a swap | Knocks back enemies and provides brief invincibility |
| **Sync Gauge** | Builds up during successful attacks | Unlocks devastating ultimate abilities for the squad |

> **💡 Tip:** Always initiate a swap immediately after a heavy attack. This cancels the "wind-down" animation and keeps the momentum of your assault going without leaving you vulnerable.

## Morbid Metal Wikipedia: Character Forms and Classes

### The Vanguard (Starter Form)
The Vanguard is your most reliable all-rounder. Equipped with a high-frequency blade, this form excels at single-target damage and parrying. It is often the "glue" that holds a squad together during the early stages of a run.

### The Colossus (Heavy Support)
When the screen becomes cluttered with projectiles and smaller robots, the Colossus is your best defense. With high armor stats and a massive hammer, it can clear entire waves with a single grounded slam.

### The Shadow (Speed Specialist)
The Shadow form is designed for players who prefer high-risk, high-reward gameplay. It has the lowest health pool in the game but boasts the highest critical hit rate and movement speed. Use the Shadow to dash behind shielded enemies and deliver lethal backstabs.

## Combat Tips for Surviving the Wasteland

Mastering the flow of combat is what separates a successful run from an early grave. The enemies in this world are relentless and will often coordinate their attacks to trap you in a corner. 

*   **Prioritize Ranged Units:** Always take out drone-type enemies first. Their chip damage can ruin a perfect run by preventing your health from regenerating between encounters.
*   **Parry, Don't Just Dodge:** While dashing is safer, a successful parry with the Vanguard form restores energy to all other forms in your squad.
*   **Manage Your Sync Gauge:** Don't waste your ultimate ability on standard mobs. Save it for the "Elite" encounters or the final boss of each biome.
*   **Environmental Awareness:** Many arenas contain explosive canisters or electrical traps. Use the Colossus to knock enemies into these hazards for "free" damage.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when swapping forms while air-borne. Some heavier forms have a fast fall speed that can drop you directly into environmental hazards like lava or bottomless pits.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I unlock new Metal forms in Morbid Metal?**

A: New forms are typically unlocked by defeating specific bosses at the end of biomes. Once a boss is defeated for the first time, you will receive their "Core," which can be activated at the hub to add that form to your rotation.

**Q: What happens when one of my forms dies during a run?**

A: If a single form's health reaches zero, it becomes "Corrupted" and cannot be used for the remainder of that combat encounter. It will slowly self-repair over time as you deal damage with your remaining forms. If all four forms are destroyed, the run ends.

**Q: Is there a limit to how many times I can swap characters?**

A: There is no hard limit on the number of swaps, but each swap consumes a small amount of "Swap Energy." This energy regenerates quickly while attacking or moving, but mindless spamming will eventually leave you unable to switch during a critical moment.
