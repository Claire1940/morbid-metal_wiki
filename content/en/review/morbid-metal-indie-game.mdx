## The Core Combat: Character Swapping and Corpora

The defining feature of this title is the ability to swap between three distinct characters (known as Corpora) at any time during a run. Each character shares a single health bar but maintains separate ability cooldowns and attack gauges. Mastering the timing of these swaps allows for devastating combos and provides a safety net when one character's abilities are on cooldown.

## Progression Systems: How to Power Up

Progress in the **morbid metal indie game** is split between temporary in-run buffs and permanent meta-progression. Understanding how to balance these resources is vital for reaching the final bosses of the two primary biomes.

### 1. In-Run Upgrades (Routines)
Routines are picked up after clearing combat arenas. These modify your character's active abilities. For example, you might find a Routine that adds electrical discharge to Flux’s Nano Blades or one that increases the armor duration for Equi.

### 2. Run Buffs (Corpora Collectibles)
Not to be confused with the characters themselves, these collectibles provide passive stat boosts for the duration of your current run.
*   **Health Augments:** Increases your shared health pool.
*   **Damage Multipliers:** Scales your base attack power.
*   **Skill Haste:** Reduces the cooldown of your special attacks.

### 3. Permanent Upgrades (The Void)
Between runs, you return to The Void, a safe haven overseen by the enigmatic figure Eden. Here, you spend **Void Points** earned during combat to unlock permanent stat increases and new character options.

| Upgrade Category | Effect | Cost Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stat Nodes** | Permanent HP and Attack boosts. | **High** |
| **Shrine Frequency** | Increases the spawn rate of healing shrines. | **Medium** |
| **New Corpora** | Unlocks additional characters for your roster. | **Very High** |
| **Lore Fragments** | Unlocks backstory regarding the Operator and Eden. | **Low** |

## Navigating the Simulation: Biomes and Enemies

The simulation is currently divided into two massive biomes, with the **Sublime Gardens** serving as the primary introductory area. Each biome consists of a series of interconnected arenas. While the pathing is largely linear, secret paths often lead to hidden chests containing rare Corpora buffs.

### Enemy Archetypes
The **morbid metal indie game** features over 10 distinct enemy types ranging from fodder drones to massive, screen-filling bosses.
*   **Ranged Drones:** These hover above the battlefield and fire slow-moving projectiles. They should be your first priority.
*   **Melee Sentinels:** Heavily armored units that require parrying or heavy attacks from Equi to stagger.
*   **The Operator's Mistakes:** Rare elite units that appear in challenge rooms, offering high Void Point rewards.

## Advanced Strategies for 2026

To truly excel, players must look beyond basic button mashing. The game’s mechanics reward precise timing and resource management.

### Managing Animation Locks
One of the most significant challenges for new players is the "animation lock" that occurs after using a special ability. During this window, your character is vulnerable to enemy attacks. 
> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not trigger Flux’s special attack if a boss is preparing a ground-slam. You cannot dodge until the animation completes, which often leads to unavoidable damage.

### Optimization and Performance
As of the 2026 updates, the game runs smoothly on most PC setups, but Steam Deck users should exercise caution. For the best experience on handheld:
1.  **Use Remote Play:** If possible, stream the game from a powerful PC to avoid the "fuzzy" graphics associated with native low-spec rendering.
2.  **Adjust Control Input:** Ensure your deadzones are minimized to help with the game's high-speed dodging requirements.

## FAQ

**Q: Is Morbid Metal a true indie game or a Ubisoft title?**

A: It is a **morbid metal indie game** at its heart, developed by the small team at Screen Juice (originally started by Felix Shade). Ubisoft acts as the publisher, providing financial backing and marketing support to help the small team polish the final product.

**Q: How many characters can I play in the current version?**

A: As of the early 2026 builds, there are three playable Corpora characters available. Each has a unique set of four abilities and a specialized playstyle, ranging from high-speed melee to heavy tanking.

**Q: How long does a typical run take?**

A: A successful run to the first major boss typically takes about 20 minutes. If you choose to explore every optional challenge room for extra Void Points, a full run can extend to 45 minutes or more.

**Q: Is there a story in Morbid Metal?**

A: Yes. You play as an AI tasked by "The Operator" to eliminate rogue simulations. Throughout your journey, you interact with a giant, enigmatic woman named Eden who provides lore fragments and upgrades, slowly revealing the truth behind the collapsing world.
