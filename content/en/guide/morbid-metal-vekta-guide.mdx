## How to Unlock Vekta (Vector)

Unlike the starting operators, Vekta must be found and activated within the simulation. You will typically encounter this character during the second stage of a run. After clearing a specific combat arena, you will find a deactivated chassis. Upon interacting with it, you will unlock the ability to shapeshift into Vekta mid-combat.

> **⚠️ Warning:** In the current 2026 Early Access build, Morbid Metal does not feature a checkpoint system. If you die before completing the run, you must restart from the beginning, though Vekta will remain unlocked for future iterations once the initial activation is complete.

## Vekta Ability Breakdown

Vekta plays fundamentally differently than the melee-focused operators. His primary fire consists of rapid-fire shurikens, allowing you to maintain a safe distance from "Hammerhead" elites and toxic environmental hazards. This **morbid metal vekta guide** recommends using him primarily for crowd control and picking off healers.

| Ability Key | Name | Description | Combat Utility |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Q** | **Magnetic Pull** | Drags lightweight enemies toward Vekta and applies a brief stun. | Perfect for grouping enemies before swapping to a melee operator. |
| **E** | **Kinetic Blast** | A powerful force push that knocks ground enemies backward. | Use this to shove enemies into acid or toxic water for instant kills. |
| **R** | **Shining Toss** | Throws a spinning shuriken that lingers in place for several seconds. | Provides massive continuous damage against stationary targets or bosses. |
| **Ultimate** | **Massive Shuriken** | A giant projectile that creates a vortex, pulling in all nearby mobs. | Clear entire rooms of minions instantly with this high-charge ability. |

## Understanding the Linked Condition

One of the most powerful aspects of Vekta’s kit is the **Linked Condition**. When Vekta hits multiple enemies with specific attacks, they become linked. Subsequent attacks will bounce between these enemies, effectively multiplying your damage output against groups.

*   **Bounce Damage:** While bounces deal slightly less damage than the initial hit, the sheer frequency of hits makes it the best tool for dealing with "Drone" swarms.
*   **Synergy:** Apply the Linked condition with Vekta, then swap to a heavy hitter like Flux to capitalize on the grouped-up, debuffed enemies.

## Upgrade Path and the Emporium

Throughout your run, you will collect **Mana Chips**. These are spent at the Emporium, which appears periodically between combat sectors. To maximize Vekta's effectiveness, you should prioritize upgrades that enhance his range or cooldown reduction.

| Upgrade Type | Priority | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Ability Level 2/3** | **High** | Increases the damage and pull strength of Magnetic Pull. |
| **Health Max** | **Medium** | Essential for surviving the "Prophet" boss's heat-seeking orbs. |
| **Leak (Bleed)** | **Very High** | Adds damage-over-time to shuriken hits, allowing for "run-and-gun" tactics. |

## Boss Strategy: Defeating the Prophet

The Prophet is the second major hurdle in Morbid Metal. This boss utilizes long-range heat-seeking orbs and devastating dash attacks. Vekta is the MVP for this fight due to his ability to deal damage while staying mobile.

1.  **Phase 1 (Orbs):** Stay on the move. Use Vekta's shurikens to chip away at the boss while running in wide circles to avoid the seeking projectiles.
2.  **Phase 2 (Pillars):** When the boss prepares a dash, hide behind the stone pillars. If the Prophet hits a pillar, he will be stunned, allowing you to use **Shining Toss** for maximum damage.
3.  **Phase 3 (Survival):** In the final phase, the boss will spawn multiple minions. Do not focus entirely on the boss; use Vekta's Ultimate to clear the minions. Interestingly, the Prophet often takes damage from his own chaotic environmental abilities in this phase—your primary job is to survive.

> **💡 Tip:** If you experience a bug where shapeshifting prevents you from dodging, try to reset your animation by jumping or using a basic ability. This is a known issue in the 2026 Early Access version.

## FAQ

**Q: Is Vekta better than the starting melee operators?**

A: Vekta isn't necessarily "better," but he is specialized. He excels at range and crowd control, whereas the starting operators deal higher burst damage in melee range. A successful **morbid metal vekta guide** strategy involves swapping between them frequently.

**Q: How do I deal with the Hammerhead enemies using Vekta?**

A: Do not let them get close. Use **Kinetic Blast (E)** to keep them at bay and use **Magnetic Pull (Q)** only if you plan to immediately swap to a melee character to finish them off.

**Q: What should I do if my ultimate ability won't trigger?**

A: This is a rare bug in the current 2026 build. Usually, performing a "Perfect Dodge" or taking a small amount of damage will reset the character state and allow abilities to fire again.

**Q: Can I use Vekta's Kinetic Blast on bosses?**

A: Bosses are generally immune to the knockback effect of Kinetic Blast, but it still deals a small amount of impact damage and can help clear away smaller minions that the boss summons.
