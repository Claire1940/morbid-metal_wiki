## The Void Hub: Your Base of Operations

Before diving into specific combat tactics, you must understand the central hub known as the Void. This is your de facto home between runs where you can interact with various terminals to improve your performance. There are two primary resources you will gather during your simulations: standard currency and Void Matter.

Standard currency is typically used for altering starting abilities for each character, allowing you to tailor your playstyle. Void Matter, however, is the more precious resource. It is used in the Void Nexus to provide permanent stat boosts that persist across all future runs.

| Resource Type | Primary Use | How to Acquire |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard Currency** | Swapping and altering character abilities | Dropped by enemies and found in chests |
| **Void Matter** | Permanent stat upgrades in the Void Nexus | Reward for clearing biomes and trials |
| **Enhancers** | Limited-time buffs and passive upgrades | Purchased from vendors between biomes |
| **Corporas** | In-run passive stat gains (e.g., Air Damage) | Found in hidden platforming sections |

> **💡 Tip:** Prioritize gathering Void Matter early in your playthrough. Even a failed run is a success if you manage to bring back enough matter to increase your base health or damage.

## The Void Nexus: Permanent Stat Upgrades

The Void Nexus is where the bulk of your long-term progression occurs. These upgrades apply to all characters—Flux, Iku, and Vecta—making them the most efficient use of your time. When deciding where to allocate your points, consider your current struggle. If you find yourself dying too quickly, health and armor are obvious choices, but in a fast-paced action game like Morbid Metal, offense is often the best defense.

### Recommended Stat Priority

1.  **Basic Attack Damage:** This is the foundation of your DPS. Since abilities have cooldowns, your basic attacks fill the gaps.
2.  **Attack Speed:** Faster attacks mean you can apply status effects like "Leak" much more quickly.
3.  **Critical Damage:** Essential for late-game builds where your critical hit chance is bolstered by in-run perks.
4.  **Health/Armor:** Useful for survivability, but less critical if you master the dodge and counter mechanics.

| Stat Name | Effect per Level | Recommended Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Basic Attack Damage** | Increases raw damage of all light/heavy attacks | **High** |
| **Attack Speed** | Reduces animation frames for faster combos | **High** |
| **Critical Damage** | Multiplies damage dealt on critical hits | **Medium** |
| **Critical Chance** | Increases frequency of critical strikes | **Medium** |
| **Max Health** | Increases the total damage you can sustain | **Low** |

## Character Abilities and Customization

A core component of any **morbid metal upgrade guide** is understanding how to cycle between your characters. You start with Flux, but you will eventually command a squad of three. Each character has a unique role, and their abilities can be swapped at the Void hub to create specialized builds.

### Flux: The Agility Specialist
Flux is your starting warrior, wielding a katana with precision. Flux excels at single-target damage and mobility. His teleporting stabs allow you to close the gap instantly, while his slicing projectiles provide much-needed mid-range options.
*   **Key Upgrade:** Look for ability permutations that allow his projectiles to bounce between enemies or leave "echoes" that explode for secondary damage.

### Iku: The Powerhouse
Iku is the heavy hitter of the group. With a focus on stunning enemies and launching them into the air, Iku is perfect for breaking through the defenses of shielded foes.
*   **Key Upgrade:** Focus on upgrades that increase stun duration or add a mid-air smash to his double-slash combo.

### Vecta: The Crowd Controller
Vecta arrives later and provides tactical utility. Her ability to "link" enemies allows them to share damage, making her indispensable for large groups.
*   **Key Upgrade:** Swap her ultimate to the levitation field to keep enemies incapacitated while you cycle back to Flux or Iku for the kill.

| Character | Role | Primary Status/Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Flux** | DPS / Mobility | High Crit / Teleportation |
| **Iku** | Tank / Breaker | Stun / Launching |
| **Vecta** | Support / CC | Link / Shared Damage |

## Maximizing Your Build with the Morbid Metal Upgrade Guide

To truly succeed in Morbid Metal, you must look beyond raw stats and consider status effect synergies. Currently, the "Leak" status effect is widely considered the most powerful tool in an AI's arsenal.

### The Power of the Leak Status
Leak functions similarly to a "bleed" mechanic in other RPGs. It deals damage over time and can be upgraded to stack up to nine times. When an enemy is suffering from max Leak stacks, they take significantly more damage from all sources.

> **Warning:** Relying too heavily on the Leak status can make combat feel trivial, potentially bypassing boss phases entirely. If you are looking for a challenge, you may want to limit your Leak upgrades, but for those struggling to clear the third biome, it is your best friend.

### Synergizing Character Swaps
You can cycle between characters mid-fight, and a pro tip is to use one character to setup the status effects and another to capitalize on them. For example:
1.  Start with **Vecta** to link a group of enemies.
2.  Switch to **Iku** to launch the linked enemies into the air.
3.  Finish with **Flux** using a whirlwind attack to apply Leak stacks to the entire airborne group.

## Exploration and In-Run Upgrades

While permanent upgrades are vital, your success within a specific run depends on how you navigate the biomes. Morbid Metal features a node-based traversal system similar to *Returnal*, where you must choose your path carefully.

### Finding Corporas and Healing
Corporas are passive stat gains found within the levels. They often require light platforming or exploration off the beaten path. Unlike Void Nexus upgrades, these are lost upon death, but they provide massive boosts to things like "Air Damage" or "Cooldown Reduction."

### Trials and Risk/Reward
Occasionally, you will encounter Trials. these require you to survive for a set duration or switch forms every five seconds. The payout is usually high-tier currency or rare enhancers. If your build is feeling weak, these trials are the best way to catch up, though they carry a high risk of ending your run prematurely.

| Feature | Description | Risk Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Trials** | Survival or form-switching challenges | **High** |
| **Branching Paths** | Choice between extra rooms or immediate boss | **Medium** |
| **Hidden Nodes** | Platforming sections containing Corporas | **Low** |
| **Monkey Paw Deals** | Powerful buffs with a significant drawback | **Very High** |

## FAQ

**Q: What is the fastest way to farm Void Matter?**

A: The most efficient way to farm Void Matter is to focus on clearing the first two biomes and completing any Trials you encounter. Even if you aren't ready for the final boss, the rewards from the early stages provide enough matter to consistently upgrade your stats in the Void Nexus.

**Q: Can I reset my upgrades in the Void Nexus?**

A: Currently, there is no "respec" option for permanent stat upgrades. However, since you can eventually max out all stats, your initial choices won't permanently ruin a save file. It is still recommended to follow the priority list in this **morbid metal upgrade guide** to make your early hours easier.

**Q: How do I unlock Vecta?**

A: Vecta is unlocked by progressing through the simulation. You will typically encounter the requirements to bring her online after successfully navigating through the second biome for the first time.

**Q: Is the Leak status effect bugged?**

A: It isn't bugged, but it is currently very strong. It stacks up to nine times and can significantly increase the damage enemies take, which allows players to "phase" bosses (skip certain mechanics by dealing damage too quickly). It is a legitimate strategy for clearing difficult content.
