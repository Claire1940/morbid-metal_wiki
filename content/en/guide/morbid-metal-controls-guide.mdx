## PC Keyboard and Mouse Bindings

For many players, the precision of a mouse and the accessibility of a keyboard make it the preferred way to play. The game utilizes a standard WASD movement scheme but layers complex skill triggers and shapeshifting shortcuts on top of it. 

| Action | Keybinding | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Move Forward/Back/Left/Right** | W / S / A / D | Standard character movement. |
| **Basic Attack** | Left Mouse Button | The primary combo starter for all forms. |
| **Skill 1** | Q | A character-specific tactical ability. |
| **Skill 2** | E | A secondary ability, often for crowd control. |
| **Special Skill** | R | A high-damage ultimate with a longer cooldown. |
| **Dodge / Dash** | Spacebar | Essential for avoiding incoming strikes. |
| **Shapeshift (Next/Prev)** | Mouse Wheel / 1-4 | Instantly swap between your active metal forms. |
| **Interact** | F | Open chests, activate trials, or talk to NPCs. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep your finger near the Spacebar. Morbid Metal is heavily reliant on skill-based gameplay, and missing a single dodge can result in a quick death during high-intensity trials.

## Shapeshifting and Combat Flow

The core of Morbid Metal's gameplay is the ability to shapeshift between different characters, each possessing a unique move set and weight class. Understanding how to switch forms mid-combo is the difference between a novice and a master.

### Understanding the Forms
There are two primary archetypes you will encounter early in your journey: **Flux** and **Echo**. Flux focuses on extreme speed and rapid strikes, making it perfect for clearing out smaller enemies or closing gaps. Echo, conversely, is a heavy hitter. While slower, Echo’s attacks can stagger larger foes and deal massive burst damage.

To maximize your efficiency, you should initiate combat with Flux to build up momentum, then shapeshift into Echo to deliver a finishing blow to armored targets. The transition is nearly instantaneous, allowing for "switch-combos" where one character's skill sets up the next character's attack.

### Advanced Combat Strategy: The Backstab Loop
One of the most effective strategies involves the "Backstabbing" upgrade. By focusing your build on critical hits from behind, you can effectively erase entire squads. If you combine this with an upgrade that resets your skill cooldown upon a kill, your character will automatically zip to the next lowest-health enemy, creating a lethal chain reaction.

## Progression and Skill Synergies

As a roguelike, your control over the character grows as you collect upgrades during a run. These upgrades often modify how your buttons function. For example, some perks might add an elemental shockwave to your dodge or allow your Special Skill to heal you based on damage dealt.

When choosing upgrades, look for those that complement your preferred shapeshifting rotation. If you find yourself using Flux more often, prioritize speed and cooldown reduction. If you prefer the raw power of Echo, look for armor penetration and stagger bonuses. You can track your current build and active buffs in the [official Morbid Metal Steam community](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1866130/Morbid_Metal/) to see how other players are optimizing their runs.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I customize the keybindings in Morbid Metal?**

A: Yes, the game allows for full remapping of both keyboard and controller inputs. If you find the default **morbid metal controls guide** layout uncomfortable, you can adjust it in the Settings menu under the 'Controls' tab.

**Q: How do I unlock new metal forms to shapeshift into?**

A: New forms are typically unlocked by defeating bosses or completing specific trials within the roguelike loop. Once unlocked, you can equip them at the hub world before starting a new run.

**Q: Is there a parry mechanic in the game?**

A: While the focus is primarily on dodging and overwhelming enemies with speed, certain metal forms have skills that function as counters or blocks. Mastering the timing of these specific skills is crucial for high-level play.

**Q: Why does my character sometimes go invisible when I shapeshift?**

A: This is a known bug in the Early Access version of the game. It usually occurs during rapid switching in hardware-constrained environments like the Steam Deck. It typically resolves itself after a few seconds or another shapeshift.
