## Shapeshifting and Engine Load

The core mechanic of the game—swapping between Flux, Echo, and Vector—is not just a cosmetic change. Pressing the swap button instantly fractures the character's physics and recalibrates attack arcs. From a technical perspective, this means the engine is constantly loading and unloading different asset sets and collision data.

1.  **Flux:** High close-quarters aggression. His heavy strikes generate significant particle effects upon impact with armored enemies, which can cause frame drops on older GPUs.
2.  **Echo:** Ranged pressure. Controlling space with projectiles requires the engine to track multiple active entities, increasing the load on the CPU.
3.  **Vector:** Tactical pivot. His high-mobility "blink" style movements test the engine's ability to render new environmental assets quickly as you zip through the corridors.

If you find that swapping characters causes a brief "hitch" or freeze, it is likely that your storage drive is struggling to keep up. Installing the game on an NVMe SSD is almost mandatory to maintain the "dance through chaos" that the developers intended.

## Steam Deck & Handheld Performance

For those looking to take the simulation on the go, the **morbid metal system requirements** present a unique challenge for the Steam Deck. While the game is functional on handhelds, it is clearly not yet fully optimized for the platform's custom APU.

*   **Current State:** To maintain a playable 30-40 FPS, most settings must be dropped to "Low."
*   **Battery Life:** High power draw due to the Unreal Engine rendering; expect around 90-120 minutes of playtime on a full charge.
*   **Resolution Issues:** Some players report that the UI text can be difficult to read on the smaller screen, and the resolution occasionally defaults to a non-native aspect ratio.

> **Tip:** Enable FSR (FidelityFX Super Resolution) in the game menu to gain an extra 10-15% performance boost on handheld devices.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I run Morbid Metal on an integrated GPU?**

A: It is not recommended. While the game might launch on modern integrated graphics (like Radeon 780M), the performance will likely fall below 30 FPS, making the fast-paced shapeshifting combat nearly impossible to manage.

**Q: Why does the game stutter during boss fights?**

A: Stutters are often caused by the high volume of particle effects and the "cinematic slowdown" mechanic. Lowering your Particle Quality and ensuring the game is installed on an SSD can help mitigate these issues.

**Q: Does the game support ultrawide monitors?**

A: Yes, the game supports ultrawide resolutions, though some players have reported UI stretching or resolution resets when switching between windowed and full-screen modes.

**Q: Are the morbid metal system requirements expected to change?**

A: As the game progresses through its early access roadmap in 2026, the requirements may be refined. Optimization patches typically lower the burden on the CPU, but new, more detailed environments could increase GPU demand.
