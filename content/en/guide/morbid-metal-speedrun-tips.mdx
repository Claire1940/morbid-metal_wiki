## Fundamental Movement and Traversal Mechanics

In a speedrun, every second spent walking is a second wasted. Morbid Metal rewards players who can maintain momentum between combat arenas. The primary method of movement optimization involves dash-canceling and utilizing the specific movement traits of your active robot.

To maximize velocity, you should cycle through your characters to exploit their unique dash cooldowns. Some characters possess a "heavy" dash that covers more ground but has a longer recovery, while others offer rapid-fire blinks. By switching characters mid-dash, you can often preserve forward inertia while resetting the dash timer of the previous unit.

| Movement Technique | Execution | Speed Increase |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Dash Shifting** | Switch characters at the apex of a dash | **High** |
| **Edge Clipping** | Dash precisely at the corner of geometry | **Medium** |
| **Jump Buffering** | Input jump frames before landing from a dash | **Low** |

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep a high-mobility character in your rotation specifically for traversal segments. This prevents you from wasting the cooldowns of your primary damage dealers before entering a boss arena.

## The Core System: Optimizing Damage Output

The key to "melting" bosses, as seen in top-tier 2026 runs, lies in the Core system. Cores are the primary upgrade path in Morbid Metal, and choosing the right combination can lead to exponential damage growth. For a speedrun, you should prioritize Cores that offer "On-Switch" bonuses or flat damage multipliers that reward aggressive playstyles.

Research into the current version of the game suggests that stacking damage-per-second (DPS) Cores is significantly more effective than defensive options. If you can eliminate a boss before they finish their first attack animation, you negate the need for health or armor upgrades entirely.

| Core Type | Speedrun Priority | Primary Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Overclock Core** | **Critical** | Massive damage boost during character shifts |
| **Kinetic Core** | **High** | Increases damage based on movement speed |
| **Flux Core** | **Medium** | Reduces character swap cooldowns |

To execute a phase skip, you must synchronize your ultimate abilities with your character swaps. By layering the "Overclock" status from multiple characters simultaneously, the boss's health bar can be depleted before the game logic triggers the next phase. ## Character Synergies for Speedrunning

Not all characters are created equal when it comes to time efficiency. A standard speedrun team usually consists of one "Enabler," one "Burst DPS," and one "Finisher." 

1. **The Enabler:** This character usually applies debuffs to the enemy or buffs the player's next switch.
2. **The Burst DPS:** This character is brought in when the boss is vulnerable. They utilize high-cooldown abilities to chunk the health bar.
3. **The Finisher:** A character with high execution damage or fast basic attacks to clean up the remaining health without wasting major cooldowns.

> **Warning:** Be careful not to swap too early. If you swap before an animation completes, you might lose the damage frames of a powerful ability, effectively ruining the phase skip attempt.

## Advanced Morbid Metal Speedrun Tips: RNG Management

While Morbid Metal features procedural elements, a successful speedrun relies on mitigating RNG (Random Number Generation). This involves knowing which rooms to clear and which to ignore. In most cases, you should only stop to fight if the room is "locked" or if the reward is a guaranteed Core upgrade that fits your build.

Follow these steps to manage your run efficiency:
* **Identify the Exit:** Learn the tile-set patterns to predict where the exit portal will spawn.
* **Skip Optional Combat:** Use your dash-shifting to bypass non-essential enemy mobs.
* **Prioritize Shops:** If you have the currency, shops are faster than combat encounters for obtaining specific Cores.
* **Reset Early:** If the first two rooms do not provide a damage-oriented Core, it is often faster to reset the run than to struggle with low DPS.

| Room Type | Speedrun Action | Reason |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Combat Arena** | **Skip** | High time cost, low reward unless locked |
| **Treasure Room** | **Enter** | Fast power spike with minimal time loss |
| **Elite Encounter** | **Assess** | Only engage if a specific Core is needed |

## FAQ

**Q: What are the best morbid metal speedrun tips for beginners?**

A: Focus on learning the dash-shift mechanic first. Being able to move quickly through levels is more important than perfect combat in the early stages. Once you are comfortable with movement, start practicing boss "melts" by stacking damage Cores.

**Q: How do I skip the second boss's second phase?**

A: You need to deal massive burst damage right before the health threshold for the second phase is reached. Use characters that have high "On-Switch" damage bonuses and ensure your Overclock meter is full before the fight begins.

**Q: Which character is the fastest for traversal?**

A: Typically, the lighter, assassin-style robots have the fastest dash recovery and the longest horizontal travel distance. However, combining dashes from multiple characters via shifting is always faster than using just one.

**Q: Are Cores really that important for a speedrun?**

A: Yes, Cores are the backbone of your damage output. Without specific damage-multiplying Cores, you will not have enough DPS to skip boss phases, which will significantly increase your overall time. Focus on Cores that reward switching and high-speed movement.
